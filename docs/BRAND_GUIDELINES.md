# BRAND GUIDELINES

This document outlines the brand positioning, visual rules, and editorial identity of **Intent Studio** exactly as currently implemented.

---

## 1. Brand Positioning

Intent Studio is an elite, focused Web & Product Design Studio. It builds launch-ready digital experiences and robust visual systems for founders, startups, and ambitious growing brands.

### Core Service Offerings
1. **Web Design & Development:** Driving conversions through clear, high-intent landing pages, business platforms, and custom-styled SaaS sites.
2. **Product & UX Design:** Designing clean, developer-ready applications, detailed user flows, and structured interface systems.
3. **UX Auditing & Conversion Optimization:** Uncovering hidden trust, messaging, and structural design leaks that cause businesses to quietly lose enquiries and sales.

---

## 2. Brand Personality & Tone

### Minimalist Credibility
- **Humble Professionalism:** We avoid hype, sales pitches, exclamation marks, or dramatic branding (e.g. we do not use self-praise like "groundbreaking", "jaw-dropping", or "revolutionary").
- **Literal Naming:** We use simple, honest human descriptions. Features and links are named objectively (e.g., "Book a Call", "Start My Audit", "Case Study").

### Visual Persona: "Editorial Tech"
- **High-contrast, eye-safe midnight canvas:** Designed to look like a premium physical magazine meets high-end modern SaaS platform.
- **Classic Editorial Serif paired with Technical Sans-Serif:** Refined serif strokes represent deep taste, classical proportions, and luxury, while clean sans-serif bodies convey engineering excellence, speed, and precision.

---

## 3. Visual Identity Rules

### Color Code Guide
- **Dominant Tone (90%):** Pure pitch black (`#000000`).
- **Secondary Surfaces (8%):** Deep volcanic grays (`#0a0a0a`, `#111111`, `#1A1A1A`).
- **Primary Highlights (2%):** International Orange (`#F24E1E`). Used ONLY to emphasize critical phrases (e.g., "costing your business", "leaking customers") or decorative eyebrows/tags.
- **Interactive Action Elements:** Pure white (`#ffffff`) for backgrounds and text.

### Button Standards
- **Standard Button Design:** Rounded corners (`rounded-lg`), medium text weight (`font-medium`), standard text sizing (`text-sm`), and comfortable structural heights aligned to `px-6 py-3`.
- **Primary CTAs:** Solid white canvas (`bg-white`), dark slate typography (`text-black`), transitioning into a soft gray on hover (`hover:bg-zinc-200`).
- **Secondary Actions:** Deep charcoal/midnight bodies (`bg-zinc-900`), thin white/alpha boundaries (`border border-white/10`), transitioning into soft hovering states (`hover:bg-zinc-800`).

### Whitespace Philosophy
- **Negative Space is an Active Component:** We enforce generous margins and paddings to allow typography to breathe. Sections are separated with clean, thin `border-white/5` structural lines instead of clutter.
- **Asymmetric Balance:** Align layouts left-to-right to create a sense of direction, guide cursor flows, and keep content easy to scan.

---

## 4. Asset & Photography Guidelines

### Editorial Context
- All portfolio photographs and mockup showcases must adhere to a highly refined, low-exposure, dark editorial style.
- Avoid bright, colorful, or cartoonish graphics. High-fidelity renders, minimalist architectural setups, clean workspace captures, and professional UI mockups are preferred.

### Placeholder Fallbacks
- In situations where live assets are missing or during local drafts, we employ custom programmatic SVG placeholders matching the brand's exact tone (deep blues/violets with clean typographic labels) rather than broken image tags or low-contrast icons.
